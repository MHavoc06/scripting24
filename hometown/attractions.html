ATTRACTIONS

One of the most charming sights which makes Bruges so picturesque are the canals and no trip to the “Venice of the North” is complete without some sort of boat excursion in Bruges. There are five landing stages where tourists can take a half hour ride through the canals of Bruges.

The canals circle the city roughly following the historic fortifications of Bruges. Once the canals played a vital role in the transportation of goods, today only tourist boats can be seen on the water. The boats operate from March to mid-November throughout the day. There are several companies which offer the cruises, but they all have guides offering a commentary in several languages, they all last 30 minutes and they all take the same route. From the water you will get up close to a number of sites which are often overlooked on foot, and you can see the city from a new angle. The boats are all open but if it rains, they provide umbrellas. If you enjoy the canal tour of Bruges, you might want to take the riverboat cruise to the neighboring city of Damme. It is not that far away and if you are fit you can walk back to Bruges along the cycle path.
