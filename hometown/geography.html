GEOGRAPHY

The medieval architecture in Bruges is mostly intact, making it one of the best-preserved medieval towns in Europe.Renowned for its stunning architecture designs and culinary delights particularly its Belgian waffles and chocolate.

The historic Centre of the Bruges has been a UNESCO world Heritage Site since 2020. The Flemish primitives, the world-renowned Concertgebouw, a whole range of top exhibitions – Bruges is certainly not lacking in top-quality cultural attractions. But you might also enjoy joining the locals for an intimate concert on one of Bruges’ many squares, a picnic in a museum garden or marveling at the street art you come across as you wander around the city. From the spectacular to the low-key, Bruges has plenty of culture to keep you happy, regardless of whether you like to have everything meticulously planned in advance or prefer to just see what you stumble upon Bruges had a strategic location at the crossroads of the northern Hanseatic League trade, and the southern routes. Bruges has significant economic importance, thanks to its port, and was once one of the world's chief commercial cities. Bruges was known for its lace, a textile technique.
